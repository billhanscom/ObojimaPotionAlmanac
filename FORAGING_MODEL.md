# Foraging Aid Model

## Purpose

The Foraging Aid is not a random loot table. It is a resolution tool.

It models the useful potion ingredients a character successfully recognizes and collects during a foraging attempt. It does not attempt to model the full ecology or society of Obojima. Every search assumes the character is surrounded by many plants, fungi, minerals, objects, scraps, and other details that are not useful potion ingredients and are therefore not shown.

## Core Principle

The generator rewards the player's decision about where to search, while the DM's DC and the character's roll determine how successful that search was.

## Three Contributors

### 1. Player Choice: Region and Search Area

The player decides where the character searches.

This should be the strongest influence on the result. If the player searches near a river, most of the haul should feel connected to rivers or related environments. Surprising results are allowed, but they should remain believable rather than random.

### 2. DM Choice: DC

The DC is the DM's contribution. It represents the circumstances that make the search easier or harder.

In the generator, DC affects which kinds of results are more competitive:
- Lower DC favors common and expected ingredients.
- Higher DC makes uncommon, nearby, or less obvious ingredients more competitive.

The DC should not make the selected Search Area stop mattering. A high-DC river search should still feel like a river search.

### 3. Character Result: Degree of Success

The roll result is not a player decision. It is the resolution of the character's ability, knowledge, attention, and luck.

In the generator, Degree of Success affects:
- how many ingredients are found
- how likely the character is to identify less obvious or uncommon ingredients

A higher Degree of Success does not create ingredients. It represents the character doing a better job recognizing useful ingredients among the larger background of the environment.

## Rarity

Published rarity is treated as a baseline relationship:
- Common : Uncommon = 5 : 1
- Rare ingredients are currently excluded from Foraging Aid results.

Uncommon ingredients are possible, but they should feel meaningful. A well-matched Uncommon ingredient can compete with a poorly matched Common ingredient.

## Search Area Identity

The selected Search Area should dominate the character of the haul.

The generator attempts to make most results associated with the selected Search Area or a closely related environment. A small amount of surprise is allowed, but most results should reward the player's choice.

## Yatamon

Yatamon is handled differently because it is not just an ecosystem. It is a trade hub and urban environment.

Yatamon Search Areas represent processes rather than simple habitats:
- City Streets: what appears in normal urban life
- Market: what commerce leaves behind
- Waterfront: what trade, water, and shipping leave behind
- Ruins: what history and abandonment leave behind
- Shrine: what ritual, gardens, offerings, and spiritual places leave behind
- Subway: what survives in dark, damp, abandoned infrastructure

The Market does not mean the character is shopping. It means they are foraging or scavenging what the market leaves behind.

## Non-forageable Ingredients

Some ingredients are useful in the potion system but do not make sense as free foraging results. These are excluded by configuration.
